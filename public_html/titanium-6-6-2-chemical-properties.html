    Principal Design Features This is a near-beta alloy designed primarily for airframe forging applications. It is best used in applications requiring high strength (in the 180,000 tensile range). 6-6-2 combines the best hot-die forgeability of any commercial titanium with excellent high strength toughness and deep hardenabilty .Beta Transus (F+/- 25) 1460
    Titanium 6-6-2 Applications Often used in near-net shape forging, 6-6-2 is most commonly used in high strength airframe components.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Machinability As a family, titanium and its alloys have developed a mystique as a nightmare to machine. This is simply not the case. Experienced operators have compared its characteristics to those found in 316 stainless steel. Recommended practice includes high coolant flow(to offset the material's low thermal conductivity), slow speeds and relatively high feed rates. Tooling should be tungsten carbide designations C1-C4 or cobalt type high speed tools.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Forming This alloy can be hot or cold formed and will exhibit characteristics equivalent to those found in a 1/8 to 1/4 hard austenitic stainless steel.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Welding Not known for as an easily welded alloy, its only rated as "fair" in terms of weldability.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Heat Treatment Solution treat at 752-774 C(1385-1425 F) for 1 hour, water quench.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Forging Rough forge and finish at 871-927 C (1600-1700 F).
    Titanium 6-6-2 Hot Working Hot forming will reduce both the springback and required forming forces, and will increase the overall ductility of the material.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Cold Working The cold working characteristics of this alloy are similar to those found in austenitic stainless steels. In multiple forming operations, intermediate stress relieving is recommended to offset the alloy's tendency to work harden. Post-work annealing is recommended to re-attain maximum performance characteristics.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Annealing For sections up to 1/2", hold at 1500 F for 30 minutes.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Aging 482-510 C(900-950 F) for 8 hours, Air Cool.
    Titanium 6-6-2 Other Physical Props Beta Transus (F+/- 25) 1460
