  • Alloy Steel Aermet 100 (AM 100) Chemical Data


    Iron Balance
    Principal Design Features This is an alloy designed for high strength, fracture toughness and hardness.
    Alloy Steel Aermet 100 (AM 100) Applications AM 100 has been successfully employed in jet engine shafts, a variety of ordnance and ballistic components, as well as in fasteners.
    Alloy Steel Aermet 100 (AM 100) Machinability Machining this alloy is considered somewhat more difficult than machining 4340 at Rc 38. Carbide tooling is recommended at 280 to 350 SFM.
    Alloy Steel Aermet 100 (AM 100) Forming Formability by conventional methods is good in the annealed condition. The alloy behaves much like AISI 4340 steel.
    Alloy Steel Aermet 100 (AM 100) Heat Treatment Normalize by heating to 1650 F and hold for 1 hour, then air cool. For optimum softening for machining, normalize at 1650 F and overage anneal at 1250 F for 16 hours.
    Alloy Steel Aermet 100 (AM 100) Annealing For optimum softening for machining, normalize at 1650 F and overage anneal at 1250 F for 16 hours. This should result in a Rc 40 maximum hardness.
