  • Tool Steel M7 is available in strips, sheets, plates, round and square bars, seamless and welded pipes & tubes, Forged & buttweld Fittings, flanges, fastener, flat rolled bars, Wire & valves.

    Tool Steel M7 is one of the Cold Work, Medium Air Hardening type tool steels. It contains chromium, molybdenum, vanadium and manganese to promote deep hardening without quenching. This keeps distortion at a minimum upon hardening.

  • Tool Steel M7 Chemical Data


    Carbon 1.25 - 1.5
    Iron Balance
    Manganese 1.6 - 2.1
    Molybdenum 1.25 - 1.75
    Nickel 1.55 - 2.05
    Phosphorus 0.03 max
    Silicon 1 - 1.5
    Sulphur 0.03 max
    Principal Design Features This alloy is one of the Cold Work, Medium Air Hardening type tool steels. It contains chromium, molybdenum, vanadium and manganese to promote deep hardening without quenching. This keeps distortion at a minimum upon hardening.
    Tool Steel M7 Applications Typically used for thread rolling dies and other intricate die shapes.
    Tool Steel M7 Machinability As with all of the tool steel alloys the machinability of M7 is medium, similar to the low alloy steels. It has a machinability rating of about 85% that of the simple alloy water hardening tool steels (W group) which are rated at 100% for reference.
    Tool Steel M7 Forming Forming is done by means of machining or hot forging.
    Tool Steel M7 Welding M7 is an alloy steel and thus capable of being welded. Consult the alloy supplier for details.
    Tool Steel M7 Heat Treatment Preheat slowly to 1200 F then raise the temperature to 1475 F and hold for 45 to 60 minutes. Remove and air cool (the alloy is air hardening as the quench medium).
    Tool Steel M7 Forging The forging temperature range is rather narrow of A10. Forge at 1875 F down to 1650 F. Do not forge below 1600 F.
    Tool Steel M7 Hot Working Other than forging it is not usual practice to hot work this alloy. Consult the alloy supplier regarding hot working.
    Tool Steel M7 Cold Working The alloy may be cold worked in the annealed condition by conventional methods.
    Tool Steel M7 Annealing Anneal at 1425 F and very slow furnace cool at a rate of 25 F per hour or less.
    Tool Steel M7 Aging Not applicable to this alloy.
    Tool Steel M7 Tempering Temper at 400 F to 800 F. This will result in developing a hardness of approximately Rockwell C 62 for the low temperature to Rockwell C 55 for the 800 F temper.
    Tool Steel M7 Hardening This alloy hardens by heat treatment. See "Heat Treatment" and "Tempering".
    Other Comments M7 alloy has high wear resistance and medium toughness.
