  • Stainless Steel 440F is available in strips, sheets, plates, round and square bars, seamless and welded pipes & tubes, Forged & buttweld Fittings, flanges, fastener, flat rolled bars, Wire & valves.

    Stainless Steel 440F is a precipitation, age hardenable stainless steel. It's principal features are high transverse toughness, good resistance to general and stress corrosion cracking, and high strength that is developed by a single low temperature heat treatment.

    Principal Design Features 440F is a precipitation, age hardenable stainless steel. It's principal features are high transverse toughness, good resistance to general and stress corrosion cracking, and high strength that is developed by a single low temperature heat treatment.
    Stainless Steel 440F Applications This alloy has been used in aircraft components such as landing gear and structural sections, valves, shafts, and components in the petrochemical and nuclear industries.
    Stainless Steel 440F Machinability This is a tough machining stainless steel. Although it can be machined in all conditions, best results can be obtained in condition H1150M. Compared to type 304 stainless, speeds should be roughly 25 % lower for optimum tool life and finish.
    Stainless Steel 440F Forming This alloy can be formed using conventional techniques with the degree of formability being limited only by its high initial yield strength.
    Stainless Steel 440F Welding Best welded in the solution annealed condition, using 440F filler material. Both resistance and inert gas shielded methods are acceptable.
    Stainless Steel 440F Heat Treatment CONDITION A--Soak at 1700 F (927 C) and cool below 60 F (16 C) for 1 hour. CONDITION RH 950- Treat Condition A material at -100 F(-73C) for 2 hours, air warm to room temperature (within 24 hours of solution treatment). Then heat cold treated material to 950 F(510 C) for 4 hours and air cool. CONDITION H950, H1000, H1050, H1100, H1150- Soak solution treated material for 4 hours at specified temperature, air cool, CONDITION H1150M- Soak solution treated material at 1400 F (760 C) for 2 hours, air cool, then re-heat to 1150 F (620 C) for 4 hours and air cool.
    Stainless Steel 440F Forging Soak at 2150 F(1177 C) for 1 hour prior to forging. Do not work below 1750 F(954 C). All forgings should be solution treated prior to final hardening. Air cool to room temperature after forging.
    Stainless Steel 440F Hot Working 13-8 can be satisfactorily hot worked. Hot worked material must be solution annealed prior to hardening for reliable results.
    Stainless Steel 440F Cold Working Despite its high initial yield strength, this alloy can be satisfactorily cold worked.
