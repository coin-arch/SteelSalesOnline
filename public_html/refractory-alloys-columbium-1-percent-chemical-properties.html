  • Principal Design Features This alloy of columbium (niobium) is 98.5% columbium with additions of zirconium and other elements in minor amounts. The common designation for the alloy is Cb-1Zr. Also called KBI-1
    Columbium 1% Applications Typical applications are for thermal barriers, containers for liquid metals and nuclear applications. Structural properties are good to 3000 F. However in exposure to air a protective coating is needed at temperatures above 800 F.
    Columbium 1% Machinability This alloy is readily machined by conventional methods using high speed steel tooling.
    Columbium 1% Forming The alloy can be formed by standard methods without difficulty.
    Columbium 1% Welding Welding may be done using electron beam or gas tungsten-arc (GTAW) methods or resistance welding. There is a loss of ductility in the weld.
    Columbium 1% Heat Treatment Not heat treatable for hardness. See "Annealing".
    Columbium 1% Forging Forging may be done in the range of 1800 to 1200 F.
    Columbium 1% Hot Working Hot working, such as extrusion, should be done at 1950 F.
    Columbium 1% Cold Working The alloy may be cold worked with relative ease by conventional methods.
    Columbium 1% Annealing Anneal at 1850 to 2200 F.
    Columbium 1% Aging Not applicable.
    Columbium 1% Tempering Not applicable.
    Columbium 1% Hardening Hardens by cold working.
    Columbium 1% Other Comments For use at elevated temperatures in air a protective silicide or aluminide coating should be applied to the parts made from this alloy.
