    Principal Design Features AL 5050 is noted for its very good corrosion resistance and good workability properties. It is a non-heat treatable alloy.
    Aluminium 5050 Applications Is commonly used in the manufacture of refrigerator trim, coiled tubes, and builder's hardware.
    Aluminium 5050 Machinability The overall machinability of this alloy is considered as fair, especially in the annealed condition. In the cold worked conditions the machinability is improved over that of the annealed material. Use of proper lubricants during machining is advised.
    Aluminium 5050 Forming The best formability results are obtained in the annealed or H32 tempers. The alloy may still be formed, but with somewhat more difficulty, in the harder cold worked conditions.
    Aluminium 5050 Welding All of the commercial welding methods may be used satisfactorily with this alloy. Brazing, after experimental trials to develop parameters, is also possible.
    Aluminium 5050 Heat Treatment This all is not heat treatable.
    Aluminium 5050 Forging No data, although the alloy is considered forgeable - consult the alloy supplier.
    Aluminium 5050 Hot Working The alloy can be hot worked in the temperature range of 700 to 400 F.
    Aluminium 5050 Cold Working Cold working characteristics are very good, especially in the annealed temper. Bend radii of zero to 1/2 thickness are possible for sheet material up to 1/8" thick.
    Aluminium 5050 Annealing Annealing, id required because of extensive cold working, may be done at 650 F followed by air cooling.
    Aluminium 5050 Aging Not applicable to this alloy.
    Aluminium 5050 Tempering Not applicable.
    Aluminium 5050 Hardening Cold working will harden this alloy to the various H tempers, depending upon the degree (or amount) of cold worked strain imparted.
    Aluminium 5050 Other Physical Props Electrical conductivity 49% of copper.
    Aluminium 5050 Other Mechanical Shear strength values for AL 5050 are: O temper 15 ksi. H32 temper 17 ksi. H36 Temper 19 ksi.
